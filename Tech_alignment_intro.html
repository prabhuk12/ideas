Navigating the Tech Horizon

  • Understand the Landscape: Identify emerging technology "blips" and analyze their relevance.
  • Develop Our Perspective: Conduct deep dives into specific technologies to form a unified point of view.
  • Co-create Reusable Solutions: Collaborate on building software that provides value across accounts.
  • Educate and Demonstrate: Share knowledge through code, demos, prototypes, and paper read-outs.
  • Open Forum & New Ideas: Discuss other emerging topics and provide a platform for new ideas.

Developing Our Internal Tech Radar

  • The Inspiration: Industry leaders like ThoughtWorks use a "Tech Radar" to map the landscape.
  • Our Goal: To create an internal radar to answer two critical questions for any new tech:
    • Adoption: Which projects are already using this?
    • Opportunity: What new client solutions can we unlock with it?

From Exploration to Exploitation

  • Best Practices: What are the established patterns for this technology?
  • Lessons from the Trenches: What are the real-world challenges and "gotchas"?
  • Tooling & Gaps: What tooling is missing in the ecosystem? Can we build it?
  • Our USP: How can we use this to create a unique, marketable offering?
  • Evolving Thinking: How does this tech challenge our current assumptions?

Co-Creating: From Ideas to Impact

  • Ideation: Based on our USPs, what specific tools or frameworks can we co-create?
  • Inventory: What relevant work is already being done? Avoid duplicating effort.
  • Assembly: How do we assemble the right people and processes to build and deploy?

Education Through Code

  • Substance Over Flash: Prioritize clear, functional code over flashy demos.
  • Demonstrate the Core Idea: Every demo must clearly illustrate a specific principle.
  • From Theory to Practice: Translate concepts from papers into runnable code.
